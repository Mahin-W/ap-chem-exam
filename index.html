FRQ 1 — Kinetics (8 points)

The reaction between nitrogen dioxide and ozone is studied at 25°C:

NO2(g) + O3(g) → NO3(g) + O2(g)

ExperimentInitial [NO2] (mol/L)Initial [O3] (mol/L)Initial Rate of [NO3] (mol/L/s)
10.0100.0203.0 × 10−4
20.0100.0406.0 × 10−4
30.0300.0202.7 × 10−3
(a) (2 pts) Write the rate law expression for the reaction above.
Answer: Rate = k[NO2]2[O3]. Comparing experiments 1 & 2: [O3] doubles, rate doubles → first order in O3. Comparing 1 & 3: [NO2] triples, rate ×9 → second order in NO2.
(b) (2 pts) Calculate the numerical value of the rate constant and specify the units.
Answer: 3.0×10−4 = k(0.010)2(0.020) = k(2.0×10−6). k = 150 L2 mol−2 s−1
(c) (2 pts) Calculate the initial rate of the reaction if [NO2] = 0.040 mol/L and [O3] = 0.030 mol/L.
Answer: Rate = 150(0.040)2(0.030) = 150(1.6×10−3)(0.030) = 7.2 × 10−3 mol/L/s
(d) (2 pts) A student proposes this mechanism:
  Step 1: NO2 + O3 → NO3 + O2 (slow)
  Step 2: NO3 + NO3 → N2O6 (fast)
Is this mechanism consistent with the rate law from part (a)? Justify.
Answer: No. The slow step gives Rate = k[NO2][O3] (first order in NO2), but the experimental rate law is second order in NO2. The mechanism is not consistent.
FRQ 2 — Thermodynamics (6 points)

Consider the following reaction at 298 K:

CaCO3(s) → CaO(s) + CO2(g)

Given: ΔH = +178 kJ/mol,   ΔS = +161 J/(mol·K)

(a) (2 pts) Calculate ΔG for this reaction at 298 K. Is the reaction spontaneous at this temperature? Show your work.
Answer: ΔG = 178 − 298(0.161) = +130.0 kJ. NOT spontaneous at 298 K (ΔG is positive).
(b) (2 pts) At what temperature (in K) will this reaction become spontaneous? Show your calculation.
Answer: T = ΔH / ΔS = 178 / 0.161 = 1106 K. Spontaneous above 1106 K.
(c) (2 pts) Explain why the sign of ΔS is positive for this reaction. Refer to the states of matter.
Answer: ΔS is positive because CO2 gas is produced from solid reactants, increasing disorder. Gases have much higher entropy than solids.
